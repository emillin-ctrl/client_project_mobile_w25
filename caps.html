Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) Resource Website

What is CAPS?

CAPS is a mental health service at the University of Michigan dedicated to supporting students' emotional and psychological well-being. It offers a variety of clinical services, including: Group Work Sessions, Workshops, and Crisis support.

Group Work Sessions

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) at the University of Michigan offers peer-to-peer and group therapy sessions as a core part of its mental health support services.

Group Therapy Sessions

CAPS provides various group sessions each semester, which may include:

  1. Interpersonal Therapy Groups: Focused on improving relationships and communication in a supportive setting.
  2. Coping Skills Groups: Designed to teach strategies like mindfulness, emotion regulation, and stress management.
  3. Identity-Based Groups: Examples include groups for sexual orientation and gender identity (SOGI), addressing topics like identity exploration, family dynamics, and intersectionality.
  4. Support Groups: Peer-led or facilitated groups such as those through the Wolverine Support Network, which focus on creating safe spaces for sharing struggles.

Featured Resource: Togetherall

Recognizing that some students who are feeling isolated, depressed, or anxious may find it challenging to connect with our resources and verbally express their needs, Togetherall is an all online peer-supported tool available free for students.


Three steps to get started with Togetherall:

  1. Enter your U-M email address on the registration webpage
  2. Complete the short online form to create an account
  3. Verify your email address - be on the lookout for an email from Togetherall containing a verification link!

Crisis Resources

CAPS provides immediate support for students experiencing a mental health crisis. This includes in-person crisis counseling during business hours and after-hours urgent support via phone.

Steps to Access Crisis Support:

  1. During business hours: Visit CAPS in person or call their office for immediate assistance.
  2. After hours: Call the CAPS After Hours Urgent Support line (accessible through the main CAPS phone number).
  3. If needed, follow up with CAPS staff for ongoing care or referrals.
