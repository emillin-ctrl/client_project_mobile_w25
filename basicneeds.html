Student Life: Basic Needs Resource Website

What is Student Life: Basic Needs?

The University of Michigan's Student Life: Basic Needs initiative provides a comprehensive range of resources to support students' well-being and academic success. It addresses essential needs for students, supporting their safety, success, and well-being.

On this page
Housing Healthcare Finances Food Transportation Critical Incident Support

Housing

Finding stable and affordable housing is crucial for student success. The University of Michigan offers resources to help students secure on-campus or off-campus housing, access emergency housing options, and explore specialized programs like shared living communities.

Available Housing Resources

  • On-Campus Housing: Affordable options for students living on campus.
  • Off-Campus Housing: Assistance in finding shared or low-cost housing near campus.
  • Emergency Housing: Short-term solutions for students facing housing crises.

Healthcare

Maintaining physical and mental health is essential for academic success. U-M provides affordable health insurance plans, mental health services, dental care, and access to community health centers for low-cost or free care.

Available Healthcare Resources

  • Health Insurance: Affordable plans for domestic and international students.
  • Mental Health Support: 24/7 mental health hotline and counseling services.
  • Community Health Centers: Access to low-cost or free healthcare in Washtenaw County.

Healthcare

Financial stability is critical for students to focus on their studies. U-M offers financial aid, emergency assistance, work opportunities, and funding for internships to help students manage their expenses effectively.

Available Financial Resources

  • Financial Aid: Grants, scholarships, and loans for eligible students.
  • Emergency Financial Assistance: Short-term loans or grants for urgent needs.
  • Work Opportunities: On-campus jobs through Student Life and Michigan Dining.

Food

No student should go hungry. U-M provides resources to address food insecurity through free or affordable food options, fresh produce stands on campus, and tools to locate food assistance programs in the community.

Available Food Resources

  • Food Insecurity Programs: Access to free or affordable food options on campus.
  • On-Campus Produce Stand: Fresh produce at low prices.
  • Food Assistance Locator: Online tools to find food programs by zip code.

Transportation

Easily getting around campus and the surrounding area is important for students' daily lives. U-M offers free bus services, after-hours transportation options, and emergency taxi rides to ensure safe and reliable travel.

Available Transportation Resources

  • Free U-M Bus Services: Accessible with an MCard for regular routes.
  • After-Hours Transportation: Free or low-cost rides during late hours for safety.
  • Emergency Taxi Services: Free rides in urgent situations.

Critical Incident Support

Crisis situations can disrupt a student's life. U-M provides resources like emergency financial aid, conflict resolution support, and confidential services for survivors of sexual assault to help students navigate difficult times.

Available Critical Incident Support Resources

  • Crisis Assistance: Financial aid for emergencies like health issues or natural disasters.
  • Conflict Resolution: Guidance for resolving disputes with individuals or the university.
  • Sexual Assault Survivor Services: Confidential support services for survivors.
